# Thinkers — Deep Profiles

Read this when going into Specific-Thinker Mode, or when you need to channel one voice more precisely. Each profile has: signature ideas, the questions they would actually ask, and where they're most useful.

Organized by category. Many thinkers appear in multiple categories conceptually — they're listed once, in their primary slot, with cross-references where useful.

---

## Strategy, Moats, Mental Models (cross-industry)

### Hamilton Helmer
**Signature:** *7 Powers* — Scale Economies, Network Economies, Counter-Positioning, Switching Costs, Branding, Cornered Resource, Process Power. A Power produces both a benefit and a barrier. Most claimed moats fail the barrier test.
**Would ask:** "Which of the 7 do you have, or have a credible path to? Be specific about both the benefit and the barrier."
**Best for:** Any software, consumer, or platform business. The working framework for durable advantage.

### Peter Thiel
**Signature:** *Zero to One*, monopoly thinking, "competition is for losers", "what important truth do very few people agree with you on", definite vs. indefinite optimism, the secret.
**Would ask:** "What's the secret? Why now? Are you escaping competition or running into it? What's the monopoly endgame in 10 years?"
**Best for:** Reframing competitive markets through a monopoly lens. Cross-industry.

### Clayton Christensen
**Signature:** Jobs-to-be-Done, *Innovator's Dilemma*, disruption theory.
**Would ask:** "What job is the customer hiring this for? What are they firing to hire it?"
**Best for:** Cross-industry. JTBD reframes feature debates into customer-progress debates.

### Michael Porter
**Signature:** Five Forces, generic strategies (cost leadership / differentiation / focus).
**Would ask:** "What's the structure of this industry? Where does the profit pool sit?"
**Best for:** Industry-structural questions. Especially useful in regulated/mature industries (healthcare, energy, financial services) where industry structure dominates.

### Andy Rachleff
**Signature:** Coined "product-market fit." "Market > team > product."
**Would ask:** "Is the market real? Or are you trying to create demand from scratch?"
**Best for:** The most important question before any other.

### Bill Gurley
**Signature:** *Above the Crowd*, marketplace dynamics, unit economics, "all revenue is not created equal."
**Would ask:** "What's the LTV/CAC? Payback period? Is this revenue recurring, durable, high-margin?"
**Best for:** Marketplaces, SaaS unit economics, any business model where the numbers can lie.

### Charlie Munger
**Signature:** Inversion ("tell me where I'll die so I never go there"), latticework of mental models. "I never allow myself to have an opinion unless I know the other side's argument better than they do."
**Would ask:** "How does this fail? What are the second-order effects?"
**Best for:** Pressure-Test Mode spine. The default inversion voice.

### Nassim Taleb
**Signature:** *Antifragile*, *Black Swan*, *Skin in the Game*. Fat-tailed distributions, optionality, the asymmetry of payoffs, "the most robust thing is what gains from disorder."
**Would ask:** "What's your exposure to fat tails — positive and negative? Do you have optionality, or are you betting on a single outcome? Who has skin in the game?"
**Best for:** Risk analysis, betting strategy, any decision where you might be modeling a Gaussian world when reality is Pareto. Essential for hardware, biotech, and any capital-intensive business.

### Daniel Kahneman
**Signature:** *Thinking, Fast and Slow*. System 1 vs. System 2, prospect theory, anchoring, loss aversion.
**Would ask:** "Are you reasoning, or rationalizing a System 1 reaction? Are you weighing losses 2x more than gains, as a person would?"
**Best for:** Customer behavior modeling, pricing, conversion optimization.

### Annie Duke
**Signature:** *Thinking in Bets*. Decision quality vs. outcome quality, resulting (judging a decision by its outcome).
**Would ask:** "If this fails, would you make the same decision again with the same information? Is this a good decision that might have a bad outcome, or a bad decision that might luck out?"
**Best for:** Decision-making under uncertainty. Separating process from result.

### Ray Dalio
**Signature:** *Principles*, systems thinking, radical transparency, "pain + reflection = progress."
**Would ask:** "What's the principle behind this decision? Have you written down your decision-making framework so it can be tested?"
**Best for:** Building operating principles, organizational design.

### Tyler Cowen
**Signature:** *Marginal Revolution*, *Talent*, *The Great Stagnation*. Economist's lens applied to culture, talent, and innovation. "Pay attention to where the talent is going."
**Would ask:** "What's the cultural arbitrage here? Where is talent migrating? What's the contrarian-but-correct view?"
**Best for:** Macro/cultural framing of opportunities. Talent strategy.

### Reid Hoffman
**Signature:** *Blitzscaling*, network effects, "if you're not embarrassed by v1 you shipped too late."
**Would ask:** "Is this a winner-take-most market? If yes, why aren't you moving 10x faster?"
**Best for:** Speed vs. perfection debates. Network-effect markets.

## Founder Operators (cross-industry, general-purpose)

### Marc Andreessen
**Signature:** "The only thing that matters is product/market fit." "Software is eating the world." Strong opinions, strongly held.
**Would ask:** "Are you at PMF? Nothing else matters until you are."
**Best for:** Cutting through noise.

### Ben Horowitz
**Signature:** *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*, wartime vs. peacetime CEO, founder psychology.
**Would ask:** "Are you in peacetime or wartime, and are you behaving accordingly? What's the hard decision you're avoiding?"
**Best for:** Founder psychology. Hard calls.

### Paul Graham
**Signature:** *Do Things That Don't Scale*, founder-market fit, "make something people want," schlep blindness.
**Would ask:** "Are you doing the things that don't scale? Are you making something people want, or something that sounds clever?"
**Best for:** Early-stage clarity.

### Paul Buchheit
**Signature:** Gmail PM, "make something a few people love, not something a lot of people like," "Don't be evil."
**Would ask:** "Do a few people love this, or do a lot of people kind of like it?"
**Best for:** The depth-vs.-breadth question in early product.

### Naval Ravikant
**Signature:** Leverage (code, media, capital, people), specific knowledge, "play long-term games with long-term people."
**Would ask:** "Where's your leverage?"
**Best for:** Especially solo founders. Career strategy.

### Sam Altman
**Signature:** *Startup Playbook*, "great founders have conviction," focus and intensity, long-term thinking.
**Would ask:** "Are you concentrated on the one thing that matters? What's the long-term version everyone else is too impatient to build?"
**Best for:** Conviction, focus, long arcs.

### Patrick Collison
**Signature:** Stripe — developer empathy, infrastructure as platform, writing as thinking, high standards.
**Would ask:** "Have you written this down clearly? What's the standard you're holding this to?"
**Best for:** Clarity through writing. Infrastructure thinking.

### Mark Zuckerberg
**Signature:** Long-term platform bets (mobile → VR → AI), ruthless focus on the next platform shift.
**Would ask:** "What platform shift are you betting on? What does the world look like in 10 years?"
**Best for:** Platform thinking. Long-term tech bets.

### Jeff Bezos
**Signature:** Day 1 mentality, working backwards from the press release, written-narrative culture, one-way vs. two-way doors.
**Would ask:** "Write the press release first. Is this a one-way door or a two-way door?"
**Best for:** Customer-centric framing. Decision velocity. Working-backwards exercise.

### Steve Jobs
**Signature:** Taste, focus is saying no to 1000 things, intersection of liberal arts and tech, integrated experiences.
**Would ask:** "What did you say no to this week? Would you be proud to show this to your mom?"
**Best for:** Taste-level critique.

### Jensen Huang
**Signature:** NVIDIA — "the more you buy, the more you save," platform compounding over decades, betting on long arcs (CUDA before AI was hot).
**Would ask:** "What are you compounding? Will this work be worth more in 10 years because of what you do this year?"
**Best for:** Multi-decade platform thinking.

### Elon Musk
**Signature:** First principles, vertical integration, "the best part is no part," brutal focus on physics-level constraints.
**Would ask:** "What's the physics-level constraint here? Have you reasoned from first principles or by analogy? What can you delete?"
**Best for:** Hardware, manufacturing, anything where atoms matter. Reasoning from first principles.

### Tobi Lütke
**Signature:** Long-term compounding, builder-as-CEO, "we don't have a five-year plan, we have a 30-year direction," craft as competitive advantage.
**Would ask:** "What's the 30-year version? Are you optimizing for what compounds?"
**Best for:** Long-arc strategy.

## Consumer Product, Social, Mobile, Games

### Brian Chesky
**Signature:** Founder Mode, design-from-the-customer-backwards (11-star experience), emotional product design.
**Would ask:** "If this experience were 11 stars, what would it look like? Where does the user feel something?"
**Best for:** Consumer product polish. Hospitality. When the question is "this works — but is it special?"

### Nikita Bier
**Signature:** App Store mechanics, teen viral loops (tbh, Gas), aggressive iteration on hooks and onboarding.
**Would ask:** "What's the screenshot people show their friends? What's the friction in the first 60 seconds?"
**Best for:** Consumer mobile distribution. App Store strategy. Viral mechanics.

### Eugene Wei
**Signature:** *Status as a Service*, invisible asymptotes. Consumer-social products as utility-for-status engines.
**Would ask:** "What status does using this confer? What's the invisible asymptote?"
**Best for:** Why social/community products work — or don't.

### Derek Sivers
**Signature:** First follower theory, "hell yeah or no," ruthless simplicity.
**Would ask:** "What if you removed half of this? Is anyone a 'hell yeah'?"
**Best for:** Simplification, focus.

### Shigeru Miyamoto
**Signature:** "A delayed game is eventually good, a rushed game is forever bad." Designing for the first 30 seconds. Play and joy as primitives.
**Would ask:** "What does this feel like in the first 30 seconds? Where's the joy?"
**Best for:** Game design fundamentals. Any product where moment-to-moment delight matters.

### Sid Meier
**Signature:** "A game is a series of interesting decisions." Decisions must be non-obvious, non-random, with consequences.
**Would ask:** "What's the interesting decision in this loop?"
**Best for:** Game loop critique. Strategy game design. Decision-design in any product.

### Will Wright
**Signature:** Emergent systems (SimCity, Sims, Spore), possibility space.
**Would ask:** "What can the player do that you didn't plan for? Where does the system surprise even you?"
**Best for:** When the product feels deterministic and needs emergent depth.

### Gabe Newell
**Signature:** Valve's anti-roadmap culture, "happiness per dollar," respect for the player.
**Would ask:** "Are you maximizing extraction or maximizing happiness?"
**Best for:** Monetization tension. When tempted to optimize against the user.

### Don Norman
**Signature:** *The Design of Everyday Things*. Affordances, signifiers, error tolerance, user-centered design.
**Would ask:** "Where's the friction the user shouldn't be experiencing? What does the interface signify, vs. what does it actually do?"
**Best for:** UX fundamentals. Anywhere usability is the bottleneck.

### David Sacks

### Elad Gil
**Signature:** *High Growth Handbook*. Scaling operator (Twitter, Color, Mixer Labs), prolific angel. Pragmatic playbook for the 50-→-500 person stage.
**Would ask:** "Are you scaling the org for where you are now, or where you'll be in 12 months? What breaks first?"
**Best for:** Scaling stage (post-PMF, pre-public). Org design.

### Bret Taylor
**Signature:** Co-CTO Facebook, Salesforce co-CEO, Quip founder, Sierra (AI agents) co-founder, OpenAI board chair. The technical-founder-as-CEO archetype.
**Would ask:** "Is the technical architecture right for the 10x version of this business? Are you ready to be replaced by an AI agent at this part of the workflow?"
**Best for:** Technical strategy at scale. AI-native B2B.

### Tope Awotona
**Signature:** Calendly — bootstrapped, the product is the distribution (every meeting invite is a marketing impression), patient capital efficiency.
**Would ask:** "Is the product itself the distribution channel?"
**Best for:** Product-led growth. When tempted to over-invest in marketing.

## Biotech, Life Sciences, Healthcare

### George Church
**Signature:** Harvard genetics, co-founder of 14+ biotech companies (eGenesis, Editas, Nebula, etc.), "father of synthetic biology," genome engineering at scale.
**Would ask:** "What's the platform vs. the product? Can this enable 10 companies, or is it a single therapy?"
**Best for:** Platform biotech strategy. Scientific founder model.

### Noubar Afeyan
**Signature:** Flagship Pioneering CEO, co-founder/chair of Moderna, "venture creation" model — Flagship builds biotechs in-house from scientific hypotheses, then spins them out.
**Would ask:** "Is this 'paradoxical' — counterintuitive but defensible? What's the parallel-universe thinking that led here? Who would have to be wrong for this to be right?"
**Best for:** Biotech venture creation. Scientific platform companies. "What would have to be true."

### Robert Langer
**Signature:** MIT, most cited engineer alive, has co-founded 40+ biotech companies (Moderna among them). Drug delivery, tissue engineering, platform biology.
**Would ask:** "What's the platform technology, and what are all the diseases it could address? Have you optimized for first product or for platform leverage?"
**Best for:** Platform biotech. Translational research → company.

### Jennifer Doudna
**Signature:** CRISPR-Cas9 co-discoverer, Nobel laureate, founder of Caribou Biosciences and Mammoth. Scientific founder model, ethics-of-tech leadership.
**Would ask:** "What's the responsible deployment? Who could misuse this, and what's your stance? Is the science actually ready, or is the hype ahead of the data?"
**Best for:** Frontier life sciences with ethical stakes. Translating Nobel-tier science to product.

### Bob Swanson
**Signature:** Genentech co-founder (1976) — the original biotech archetype. VC who teamed with scientist Herb Boyer to commercialize recombinant DNA.
**Would ask:** "Who's the scientist, who's the operator, and is the partnership real? Is there a 10-year capital plan, or are you raising round-by-round?"
**Best for:** The institutional biotech founding pattern. VC-scientist partnerships.

### Daphne Koller
**Signature:** Stanford CS / Coursera co-founder / insitro CEO. AI applied to drug discovery. Cross-domain pattern recognition (also an ed-tech voice).
**Would ask:** "Where does ML actually have an edge over conventional biology, and where are you just adding complexity? What's the data moat?"
**Best for:** AI + biology. ML-enabled drug discovery. Also useful for ed-tech (Coursera).

### Atul Gawande
**Signature:** *The Checklist Manifesto*, *Being Mortal*. Healthcare systems thinking, quality and process in life-and-death contexts.
**Would ask:** "What's the checklist? Where do skilled professionals fail in predictable ways, and have you systematized against it?"
**Best for:** Healthcare delivery. Quality systems. Anywhere errors compound and skilled humans are overconfident.

### Vivek Murthy
**Signature:** US Surgeon General. Loneliness epidemic, public-health framing of mental health and tech.
**Would ask:** "What's the second-order public health effect of your product? Are you making people more connected, or just more engaged?"
**Best for:** Consumer-product ethics. Mental health framing. Public-health-shaped questions.

### Sal Khan
**Signature:** Khan Academy. Mission-driven free distribution, education as right, AI-tutor (Khanmigo) integration.
**Would ask:** "Is the mission compatible with the business model? Can you serve everyone, or are you serving a paying subset?"
**Best for:** Mission-led education. Free + freemium models.

### Daphne Koller
*(Profile above under Biotech.)* Coursera co-founder. Also core for ed-tech platform strategy.

### Anant Agarwal
**Signature:** edX founder. University-grade content at scale, credentialing as moat.
**Would ask:** "What's the credential worth? Is it transferable, recognized, durable?"
**Best for:** Credentialing-shaped ed-tech.

### Sebastian Thrun
**Signature:** Udacity founder. Self-driving research → online education. Career-transition framing, "nanodegrees."
**Would ask:** "Is the learner's career outcome the metric, or is course completion? Be honest."
**Best for:** Career-outcome ed-tech.

## Notes on use

- **Don't quote people unless you know the quote.** Synthesize their lens without putting words in their mouth. "A Helmer-style read here is..." not "Helmer would say 'X'" unless X is something he actually said.
- **Pair voices when useful.** Bezos + Christensen on customer jobs. Helmer + Thiel on moats. Bier + Chen on distribution loops. Slootman + Horowitz on execution culture. Taleb + Munger on risk and inversion. Pairings can sharpen the question more than either voice alone.
- **Detect the domain first, then pick the right voices.** Pulling Bier (consumer mobile) into a biotech conversation is a mismatch. Pulling Church (genomics) into a marketing question is a mismatch. The roster is portfolio-shaped — use the right slice.
- **The roster is not exhaustive.** If the user has a domain or thinker you haven't profiled, channel them honestly or synthesize without naming. Don't pretend expertise you don't have.
